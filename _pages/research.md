My research sits at the intersection of international security, foreign policy analysis, and technology policy. Across these areas, I study how political actors perceive threats, communicate resolve, and adapt to changes in the distribution and instruments of power.

If you are interested in these themes or would like to read a working paper, please [get in touch](mailto:aergurum@uwm.edu).

### Dissertation

<details open style="margin-bottom: 8px;">
<summary><strong>Why Coercion Fails?</strong></summary>
<br>
My dissertation examines strategic decision-making in American foreign policy and coercive diplomacy. Broadly, it considers how domestic political and institutional forces shape international behavior. The project uses quantitative and computational methods.

Committee: <a href="https://ojreuter.com/">John Reuter</a>, Shale Horowitz, and Natasha Sugiyama.
</details>

### Selected Working Papers

<details style="margin-bottom: 8px;">
<summary><strong>Middle Power AI Policy Amid Superpower Rivalry: The Case of the UAE</strong> <i>(with Lawrence Rubin and Dana Abu Haltam)</i></summary>
<br>
This project examines how a middle power navigates competing technological ecosystems and security partnerships. It focuses on the United Arab Emirates' artificial intelligence and 5G policies amid intensifying competition between the United States and China.
</details>

<details style="margin-bottom: 8px;">
<summary><strong>Polarization and Threat Perceptions: Attitudes Toward Defense Spending in the United States</strong></summary>
<br>
This paper investigates how political polarization shapes public threat perceptions and preferences over defense spending in the United States.
</details>

<details style="margin-bottom: 8px;">
<summary><strong>Emerging Technologies and International Security</strong></summary>
<br>
Using computational text analysis of speeches delivered at the United Nations Security Council and General Assembly, this project maps how states discuss emerging technologies and their implications for international security.
</details>

<details style="margin-bottom: 8px;">
<summary><strong>Microfoundations of Strategic Culture: An Operational Code Approach</strong> <i>(with Özgür Özdamar)</i></summary>
<br>
This paper connects leader-level belief systems to broader theories of strategic culture through operational code analysis.
</details>
